Hey! I'm Luke.

I'm studying computer science in Georgia State University's undergraduate program and working on consumer-scale automation on the side. I want to be useful in as many areas of the web stack as possible, but I've been doing primarily back-end web work and projects with hobby/IoT controllers such as the Raspberry Pi and Arduino. A number of my recent projects or components of recent projects are available on GitHub.

Presently I work with Node.js and Java, though I'm converting some personal projects to TypeScript. I've also worked in Python, C#, and (on an admittedly shallow level) C++. Language is less a barrier for me as much as my framework-specific experience, which I have yet to develop. I have done a limited amount of front-end web work with Vue.js/Webpack.

I love Major League Hacking events. Since sophomore year I've attended HackGT, UGAHacks, HackFSU, and several HackGSUs. This year, I'm looking to attend several more events as a part of a team sponsored by GSU's student organization PantherHackers.

I managed the Robotics and Programming merit badge at Woodruff Scout Reservation during the summers of 2015-2018. I was also a retail pharmacy associate for a couple years following the summer of 2015. Creating solutions to truly increase efficiency and reduce waste in the healthcare sector would be exciting.

I'd enjoy talking tech with you! Feel free to direct-message me on Twitter (@lukedsmalley) or send me an email at lukedsmalley@gmail.com. I can also be emailed through GSU at lsmalley1@student.gsu.edu.
